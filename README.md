# Natural Procreative Hygiene (NPH)

## Definition
**Natural Procreative Hygiene (NPH)** is a conceptual framework addressing the biological and ethical principles governing natural human procreative hygiene. It encompasses the study of physiological, behavioral, and normative dimensions of human reproduction as they relate to hygienic practice and ethical conduct.

## Core Principles
1. **Physiological Integrity:** Recognition of the human reproductive system as a coherent biological structure with inherent hygienic requirements.
2. **Ethical Coherence:** Alignment of procreative practices with ethical frameworks derived from natural function rather than circumvention of it.
3. **Preventive Orientation:** Emphasis on prophylactic understanding — hygiene as prevention rather than remediation.
4. **Intergenerational Responsibility:** Consideration of procreative hygiene as a duty extending across generations.

## Context
NPH provides a structured lens for analyzing the intersection of natural procreative processes and hygienic integrity, proposing that ethical and biological coherence requires adherence to principles grounded in observable natural function.
